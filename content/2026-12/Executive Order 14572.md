### Against the People’s Republic of China’s Hostile Actions Toward Taiwan

Executive Orders
September 25, 2026

By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States of America, including the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (50 U.S.C. 1701 _et seq_.) (IEEPA), the National Emergencies Act (50 U.S.C. 1601 _et seq._), section 232 of the Trade Expansion Act of 1962, as amended (19 U.S.C. 1862) (section 232), section 604 of the Trade Act of 1974, as amended (19 U.S.C. 2483), and section 301 of title 3, United States Code, it is hereby ordered:
### Section 1. Background.

In Executive Order 14389 of March 15, 2026 (Protecting American Technology Supply Chains From Coercive Practices by the People’s Republic of China), I found that the actions of the People’s Republic of China (PRC) in the semiconductor and artificial intelligence sectors constituted an unusual and extraordinary threat to the national security, foreign policy, and economy of the United States. I declared a national emergency with respect to that threat.

In late 2026, the PRC escalated destabilizing military maneuvers near Taiwan, including aerial incursions, simulated blockades, and cyber-enabled disruptions of maritime traffic management. These actions, coupled with continued industrial espionage and coercive economic practices, have increased the severity of the emergency described in Executive Order 14389.

Based on new information and recommendations from the Secretary of Commerce, the United States Trade Representative (USTR), and the Secretary of the Treasury, I have determined that it is necessary and appropriate to impose additional duties and restrictions on PRC imports and semiconductor-related entities.

---

### Sec. 2. Expanded Tariffs on PRC Imports.

(a) The USTR, in consultation with the Secretary of the Treasury and the Secretary of Commerce, shall impose ad valorem duties of 25 percent on goods of Chinese origin in the following categories:

1. Semiconductors, integrated circuits, and associated manufacturing equipment;
2. Telecommunications infrastructure, including base stations and optical transmission equipment;
3. Industrial robotics, automation systems, and components;
4. Certain strategic metals and rare earth elements.

(b) The list of goods subject to duties, including applicable tariff lines of the Harmonized Tariff Schedule of the United States (HTSUS), shall be published in Annex I to this order.

(c) These duties shall apply with respect to goods entered for consumption, or withdrawn from warehouse for consumption, on or after 12:01 a.m. eastern standard time 7 days after the date of this order.

---

### Sec. 3. Semiconductor Sanctions.

(a) The Secretary of Commerce shall add to the Entity List all PRC entities engaged in the design, fabrication, or export of semiconductors at or below the 7-nanometer process node.

(b) United States persons are prohibited from knowingly providing financing, software, design services, or technical expertise to such entities without a license issued by the Department of Commerce.

(c) The Secretary of the Treasury, in consultation with the Secretary of State, shall impose correspondent and payable-through account sanctions on any foreign financial institution determined to be facilitating significant transactions for designated PRC semiconductor entities.

---

### Sec. 4. Allied Engagement.

The Secretary of State, in coordination with the USTR, shall immediately initiate consultations with allied partners, including Japan, the Republic of Korea, Taiwan, and members of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, with the objective of securing parallel measures regarding export controls on advanced semiconductor technologies and related supply chain protections.

---

### Sec. 5. Delegation.

(a) The USTR, the Secretary of the Treasury, and the Secretary of Commerce are hereby authorized to take all necessary actions to implement this order, including through amendment of regulations, modification of tariff schedules, or publication of notices in the Federal Register.

(b) The heads of executive departments and agencies shall take all appropriate measures within their authority to carry out the provisions of this order.

---

### Sec. 6. General Provisions.

(a) Nothing in this order shall be construed to impair or otherwise affect:  
	(i) the authority granted by law to an executive department or agency, or the head thereof; or  
	(ii) the functions of the Director of the Office of Management and Budget relating to budgetary, administrative, or legislative proposals.
(b) This order shall be implemented consistent with applicable law and subject to the availability of appropriations.
(c) This order is not intended to, and does not, create any right or benefit, substantive or procedural, enforceable at law or in equity by any party against the United States, its departments, agencies, or entities, its officers, employees, or agents, or any other person.


DONALD J. TRUMP

THE WHITE HOUSE,

September 5, 2025.
