June 12, 2026
Beijing, China

Under Xi Jinping’s direction, China passes the **AI Sovereignty and Security Act (中国人工智能主权法案)**. This is the culmination of months of internal Party debate at the National People's Congress, which met in March.

### I. Strategic Context
1. The United States and its allies have escalated technological containment measures, most notably the 2025 and 2026 AI semiconductor export controls. These actions have revealed the vulnerability of our development model’s reliance on foreign compute and supply chains.
2. Following the **NVIDIA ban (Sept. 2025)** and subsequent restrictions on advanced lithography equipment, China faces a coordinated Western effort to halt our national rejuvenation.
3. Our domestic talent base has been strengthened by the return of overseas Chinese researchers and engineers, accelerated by discriminatory U.S. policies. It is now imperative to consolidate this advantage under a comprehensive security framework.

---
### II. Policy Objectives

The “AI Sovereignty and Security Act” (中国人工智能主权法案)  establishes the following national objectives:

1. **Data Sovereignty:**
    - Prohibit outbound transfer of AI training data to foreign entities.
    - Establish national data sanctuaries under state-owned cloud providers (Alibaba Cloud, Huawei Cloud).
2. **Compute Protection:**
    - Mandatory registration of all compute clusters exceeding 100 PFLOPs.
    - Export of high-performance GPUs, TPUs, and neuromorphic chips restricted under a new licensing regime.
    - Development of indigenous architecture under the “Dragon Core 2030” (龙芯2030) initiative.
3. **Talent Security:**
    - Restrictions on outbound mobility of AI experts working on frontier systems.
4. **Industrial Consolidation:**
    - Strategic cooperation of top AI labs (Baidu Research, Alibaba DAMO, Huawei Noah’s Ark, SenseTime) under joint Party control.
    - Financial incentives for private labs contingent on alignment with national objectives

---
### III. Implementation Mechanisms

1. **Central AI Security Commission (中央人工智能安全委员会)** to oversee enforcement of the AI Sovereignty and Security Act. 
2. **Great Firewall Expansion:** Algorithmic checkpoints to detect unauthorized data transfer in training pipelines.
3. **Export Control Bureau:** New office under the Ministry of Commerce to manage semiconductor export licensing.
4. **Talent Containment Measures:**
    - Revocation of passports for designated “critical personnel.”
    - Establishment of special residential zones for top-tier AI scientists to facilitate integration and monitoring.

---
### IV. Directive

All ministries are to submit detailed implementation plans within **30 days** of this memorandum. The Ministry of State Security will coordinate to ensure rapid operationalization.
